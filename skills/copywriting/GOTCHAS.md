# Copywriting Gotchas

**Last Updated:** 2026-03-17  
**Source:** Common failures from copywriting sessions

---

## 🚫 Most Common Mistakes

### 1. **Feature Dumping Instead of Benefits**
**Wrong:**
> "Our platform uses blockchain technology with smart contracts and tokenization"

**Right:**
> "Own property fractions starting at £100. Earn rental income monthly. Sell anytime."

**Why:** Features tell what it IS. Benefits tell what it DOES for the customer.

---

### 2. **Vague Claims Without Proof**
**Wrong:**
> "Industry-leading returns with minimal risk"

**Right:**
> "6-12% annual returns (based on 2024 UK property performance). Capital at risk."

**Why:** Specific numbers build trust. Vague superlatives destroy it.

---

### 3. **Jargon Without Explanation**
**Wrong:**
> "Tokenized real estate with ERC-20 compliance and SPV structuring"

**Right:**
> "Digital property ownership (like stocks, but for buildings). Each token = fractional ownership."

**Why:** Your audience doesn't know your industry terms. Explain or avoid.

---

### 4. **Multiple CTAs Competing**
**Wrong:**
> "Join waitlist, download whitepaper, book demo, follow us on Twitter"

**Right:**
> "Join waitlist → Get early access" (ONE primary CTA)

**Why:** Decision paralysis kills conversions. One action per page.

---

### 5. **Hedging Language**
**Wrong:**
> "We believe our platform could potentially help you possibly earn returns"

**Right:**
> "Earn 6-12% annual returns from UK rental properties"

**Why:** Confidence builds trust. Hedging sounds like you don't believe your own pitch.

---

### 6. **Ignoring Objections**
**Wrong:**
> (No mention of risks, concerns, or downsides)

**Right:**
> "Property investment carries risks: values can fall, tenants can default, liquidity varies. Only invest what you can afford to lose."

**Why:** Addressing objections upfront builds credibility. Ignoring them creates doubt.

---

### 7. **Generic Headlines**
**Wrong:**
> "Welcome to Our Platform" or "The Future of Real Estate"

**Right:**
> "Own UK Property from £100. Earn Monthly Rental Income."

**Why:** Generic = forgettable. Specific = memorable.

---

### 8. **No Social Proof**
**Wrong:**
> (Claims without evidence)

**Right:**
> "150+ early users. 67% conversion rate. 7 properties tokenized."

**Why:** Claims are cheap. Proof is expensive (and convincing).

---

### 9. **Wall of Text**
**Wrong:**
> (Dense paragraphs, no formatting, no whitespace)

**Right:**
> - Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences)
> - Bullet points for scannability
> - Bold key phrases
> - Clear section breaks

**Why:** People scan, they don't read. Make it skimmable.

---

### 10. **Forgetting the Mobile Experience**
**Wrong:**
> Long headlines that break on mobile, tiny CTAs, dense copy

**Right:**
> - Headlines < 60 characters
> - CTAs with thumb-friendly sizing
> - Paragraphs < 3 lines on mobile

**Why:** 60%+ of traffic is mobile. Design for thumb-scrolling.

---

## 📋 Pre-Flight Checklist

Before finalizing copy, verify:

- [ ] **ONE primary CTA** (not 3-4 competing actions)
- [ ] **Benefits > Features** (at least 3:1 ratio)
- [ ] **Specific numbers** (no vague "industry-leading" claims)
- [ ] **No jargon** (or explained if necessary)
- [ ] **Objections addressed** (risks, concerns, downsides)
- [ ] **Social proof included** (users, stats, testimonials)
- [ ] **Scannable format** (bullets, bold, whitespace)
- [ ] **Mobile-friendly** (short headlines, thumb-friendly CTAs)
- [ ] **No hedging** (confident, direct language)
- [ ] **ONE idea per section** (not trying to say everything everywhere)

## 🔁 Common Revision Patterns

### Pattern 1: "Make It Shorter"
**Before:** 500 words of dense explanation  
**After:** 150 words with bullets, bold, and whitespace

**Fix:** Cut 70%. Keep only essential info. Move details to expandable sections.

---

### Pattern 2: "Make It Clearer"
**Before:** Industry jargon, complex sentences  
**After:** Simple words, short sentences, concrete examples

**Fix:** Replace every jargon word. Split sentences > 25 words. Add examples.

---

### Pattern 3: "Make It More Persuasive"
**Before:** Feature list, vague claims  
**After:** Benefit-driven, specific proof, social proof

**Fix:** Convert features → benefits. Add numbers. Add testimonials/stats.

---

### Pattern 4: "Make It More Trustworthy"
**Before:** Hype, superlatives, no proof  
**After:** Honest, specific, addresses risks

**Fix:** Remove superlatives. Add specific data. Address risks upfront.

---

## 📊 Performance Benchmarks

**Good Copy Indicators:**
- Headline < 60 characters
- First paragraph < 3 sentences
- CTA above the fold
- 3+ social proof elements
- 1-2 risk disclosures
- 5-10 bullets for scannability

**Bad Copy Indicators:**
- Headline > 80 characters
- First paragraph > 5 sentences
- CTA buried below fold
- No social proof
- No risk disclosure
- Wall of text (no bullets/formatting)
