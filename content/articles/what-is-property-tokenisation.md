## What Is Property Tokenisation?

Property tokenisation uses blockchain technology to represent ownership of real estate as digital tokens.

Think of it like this:

**Traditional Property Ownership:**
- You buy a whole property
- You get a deed (paper document)
- You register with Land Registry
- You sell the whole property (or not at all)

**Tokenised Property Ownership:**
- A property is owned by an SPV (Special Purpose Vehicle — a UK Limited Company)
- The SPV issues tokens representing shares
- You buy tokens (not the whole property)
- Tokens can be traded (subject to regulations)

---

## How Does It Work?

### Step 1: Property Acquisition

A UK property is purchased by an SPV (Special Purpose Vehicle).

```
Property → Owned by → UK SPV (Limited Company)
```

The SPV holds legal title to the property. This is standard UK property law — nothing new here.

### Step 2: Token Issuance

The SPV issues digital tokens representing shares in the company.

```
UK SPV → Issues → Tokens (on blockchain)
```

Each token represents a percentage of ownership in the SPV, which owns the property.

### Step 3: Token Distribution

Tokens are distributed to investors (subject to FCA regulations).

```
Tokens → Distributed to → Investors (via whitelist)
```

Investors hold tokens in their crypto wallets. The tokens represent their share of the SPV.

### Step 4: Rental Income Distribution

Rent collected from the property is distributed to token holders.

```
Rent → SPV → Distributed to → Token Holders (pro-rata)
```

Distributions can be made in GBP (bank transfer) or stablecoins (USDC, etc.).

### Step 5: Secondary Trading (Optional)

Tokens may be tradable on secondary markets (subject to FCA rules).

```
Token Holder A → Sells → Token Holder B
```

Transfers are recorded on the blockchain. Whitelist ensures only verified investors can hold tokens.

## UK Regulatory Framework

### FCA Oversight

The Financial Conduct Authority (FCA) regulates cryptoassets in the UK.

**Key Regulations:**
- **CP25/28** — Fund tokenisation (consultation closed Feb 2026)
- **CP25/40** — Cryptoasset activities (Dec 2025)
- **CP25/41** — Admissions & disclosures (Dec 2025)
- **CP25/42** — Prudential regime (Dec 2025)

**Timeline:**
- **Sept 2026** — FCA "cryptoasset gateway" opens (applications)
- **Oct 2027** — Full regulatory regime in force

### Public Offers vs. Private Placements

**Public Offers:**
- Generally restricted under FCA rules
- Requires prospectus (expensive, time-consuming)
- Enhanced disclosures required

**Private Placements:**
- Allowed under exemptions
- Limited to specific investor categories
- No public solicitation

**Exemptions Include:**
- Existing shareholders/members (Founder's Pass)
- Certified high net worth individuals
- Sophisticated investors
- Small offers (under €8M, with disclosures)

### Retail Investor Protections

The FCA allows retail participation with safeguards:

- **Appropriateness assessment** — Investors must acknowledge risks
- **10% net investable cap** — Retail investors cannot allocate more than 10% of portfolio to high-risk investments
- **Clear risk disclosures** — No misleading promises
- **Express consent** — Investors must actively agree to terms

## Pros and Cons

### Potential Benefits

**Lower Barriers to Entry:**
- Traditional: £30k+ deposit required
- Tokenised: Potentially £50-100 minimum (varies by platform)

**Liquidity (Theoretical):**
- Traditional: Months to sell a property
- Tokenised: Potentially faster secondary trading (but not guaranteed)

**Diversification:**
- Traditional: One property = all capital in one asset
- Tokenised: Spread capital across multiple properties

**Transparency:**
- Blockchain records are immutable and auditable
- Ownership history is traceable

### Potential Risks

**Regulatory Uncertainty:**
- FCA regime still evolving (full rules Oct 2027)
- Rules may change, affecting existing structures

**Liquidity Risk:**
- Secondary markets may not exist or be active
- You may not be able to sell when you want

**Technology Risk:**
- Smart contract bugs (though audits help)
- Wallet security (your responsibility)
- Blockchain network issues

**Property Risk:**
- Property values can go down
- Rental income is not guaranteed
- Void periods, maintenance costs

**Complexity:**
- SPV structure adds legal layers
- Tax treatment may be complex
- Requires understanding of both property and crypto

## UK-Specific Considerations

### Stamp Duty Land Tax (SDLT)

The SPV pays SDLT on property purchase (including 3% surcharge for additional properties).

This is factored into the overall investment structure — token holders don't pay SDLT individually.

### Capital Gains Tax (CGT)

When the property is sold:
- SPV pays CGT (if applicable)
- Token holders may pay CGT on their share of gains
- Annual exempt amount: £3,000 (2026/27 tax year)

### Income Tax

Rental income distributed to token holders:
- Taxed as property income (not dividend income)
- Reported on Self Assessment tax return
- Rates: 20% (basic), 40% (higher), 45% (additional)

### Legal Structure

**Recommended:**
- UK Private Limited Company (Ltd)
- Registered at Companies House
- Directors: At least one UK resident
- Shareholders: Token holders (via nominee structure or direct)

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is property tokenisation legal in the UK?

Yes, but subject to FCA regulations. Private placements are allowed under exemptions. Public offers require prospectus or exemption.

### Do I need to be a certified investor?

Not necessarily. Retail investors can participate with appropriate safeguards (appropriateness assessment, 10% cap, risk disclosures).

### Can I lose my investment?

Yes. Property values can go down. Rental income is not guaranteed. You may lose some or all of your capital.

### Is this the same as a REIT?

No. REITs (Real Estate Investment Trusts) are listed companies with specific tax treatment. Tokenised property is direct ownership via SPV structure.

### Can I sell my tokens anytime?

Not necessarily. Secondary markets may not exist or be active. Liquidity is theoretical until proven.

### What blockchain is used?

Varies by platform. Common choices: Ethereum, Polygon, Base. The blockchain should support transfer restrictions (whitelist) for FCA compliance.

### Do I get voting rights?

Depends on the structure. Some platforms offer voting on major decisions (property sale, refinancing). Others are passive ownership only.
