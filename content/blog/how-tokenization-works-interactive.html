Key Takeaways
  • A property is purchased by an SPV (Special Purpose Vehicle) — a UK Limited Company created for that single property.
  • The SPV issues tokens representing shares — each token = fractional ownership in the property.
  • You buy tokens → you own shares in the SPV → you own a portion of the property's rent and appreciation.
  • Rent is collected monthly, expenses deducted, remaining income distributed to token holders proportionally.
  • The blockchain is just the ledger — the real innovation is fractional ownership + professional management.

What You Actually Own

This is where most guides get vague. Let's be precise:

Legal Ownership Structure
You don't own the property directly. You own shares in an SPV (Special Purpose Vehicle — a UK Limited Company) that owns the property.

Think of it like this:

Traditional Buy-to-Let Tokenized Property
You own 100% of the property (deed in your name) Shares in an SPV that owns the property
Minimum cost £50,000-£100,000 (25% deposit + costs) £500-£1,000 (tokens)
Management You (or agent at 10-15% fee) Professional management (included)
Liquidity Sell whole property (3-6 months) Sell tokens (platform dependent)
Diversification One property per £100k Multiple properties from £1k

The Blockchain Part (Simplified)

You've heard "blockchain" and thought "crypto scam." Let's demystify:

What the Blockchain Actually Does
The blockchain is just a digital ledger — like a share register for the SPV. It records: • Who owns how many tokens
• When tokens were transferred
• Rental distributions paid to each address

What it's NOT:

  • ❌ Not a cryptocurrency (you're not buying "coins")
  • ❌ Not speculative (value comes from rent + property, not hype)
  • ❌ Not volatile (property values don't swing 20% daily)

What it IS:

  • ✅ A transparent ownership record (anyone can verify)
  • ✅ Automated distributions (smart contracts pay you automatically)
  • ✅ Easier transfers (sell tokens without solicitor fees)

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I lose all my money?

Yes. Property values can fall. If the property value drops 50%, your tokens lose 50% value. If the property sits empty for months, no rent is distributed. Only invest what you can afford to lose.

How do I sell my tokens?

Phase 1 (pilot): Hold-to-rent (no secondary market yet). Phase 2 (Q4 2026): Whitelist secondary market (sell to other verified investors). Phase 3: Property sale (majority vote triggers sale + capital distribution).

Is this regulated by the FCA?

The SPV structure is standard UK company law. The FCA's new cryptoasset regime (October 2027) will require platforms to be FCA-authorised. Settley is building to be compliant from day one.

What happens if Settley goes bust?

The SPV owns the property, not Settley. If Settley fails, the SPV continues operating. A new manager would be appointed. Your ownership is not affected.

Do I pay tax on the income?

Yes. Rental distributions are taxed as property income (not dividends). Report on Self Assessment. Capital gains tax applies when you sell tokens at a profit. Consult a tax adviser for your situation.
