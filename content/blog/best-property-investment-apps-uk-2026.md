## Key Risks of Property Investment Apps

Property investment apps make real estate accessible, but they don't eliminate risk. Here's what you need to understand before investing:

### 1. Capital Risk

**You can lose money.** Property values can fall, developments can fail, and borrowers can default. Never invest money you cannot afford to lose — this is not a savings account.

### 2. Liquidity Risk

**Your money is locked in.** Most property investments cannot be withdrawn on demand. Lock-in periods range from 6 months to 3+ years. Secondary markets exist on some platforms but are often limited and may require selling at a discount.

### 3. Platform Risk

**The platform itself could fail.** Even FCA-regulated platforms can experience operational issues, management changes, or business model stress. Diversify across multiple platforms rather than concentrating all capital in one.

### 4. Interest Rate Risk

**Rising rates hurt property.** The 2022-2024 interest rate environment stressed many property platforms, particularly those focused on development lending. Higher borrowing costs reduce developer margins and can increase default rates.

### 5. Regulatory Risk

**Rules can change.** FCA regulations evolve, tax treatment shifts, and cryptoasset frameworks (for tokenized platforms) are still developing. Stay informed about regulatory changes that could affect your investments.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Are property investment apps safe?

Property investment apps regulated by the FCA must meet capital requirements, safeguard client money, and provide clear risk disclosures. However, "safe" does not mean "risk-free." You can still lose money if properties fall in value, developments fail, or borrowers default. FCA regulation protects against platform misconduct, not investment losses.

### What is the minimum I need to start investing?

The lowest minimum we found is £50 (CrowdProperty). Most platforms range from £100-1,000 for initial investments. However, some platforms like LendInvest require a £5,000 initial deposit despite having a £100 per-investment minimum.

### Can I withdraw my money whenever I want?

Generally, no. Property investments are illiquid by nature. Most platforms have lock-in periods of 12-36 months. Some offer secondary markets, but these are often limited and may require selling at a discount. Only invest money you won't need during the lock-in period.

### Are returns guaranteed?

No. Returns are targets, not guarantees. Debt investments (loans to developers) typically offer fixed interest rates, but borrowers can default. Equity investments (ownership stakes) offer variable returns based on rental income and capital appreciation — both can be positive or negative.

### Do I pay tax on property investment returns?

Yes. Rental income is taxable as income, and capital gains are subject to CGT. Some platforms offer IFISA (Innovative Finance ISA) wrappers, which allow up to £20,000 per year in tax-free returns. Check IFISA eligibility for each platform before investing.

### What happens if the platform goes bust?

FCA-regulated platforms must safeguard client money in segregated accounts. If the platform fails, your investments (the underlying properties or loans) should remain yours. However, recovering your capital may take time and could involve selling assets at unfavorable prices. This is why platform diversification matters.
