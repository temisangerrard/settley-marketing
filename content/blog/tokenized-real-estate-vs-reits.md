## What Are UK REITs?

A Real Estate Investment Trust (REIT) is a company that owns and operates income-producing property. When you buy REIT shares, you're purchasing stock in a property portfolio—not direct ownership of specific buildings.

UK REITs must meet strict criteria set by HMRC:

- Distribute at least 90% of rental income to shareholders as dividends
- Derive 75%+ of profits from property rental
- Hold property investments worth at least 75% of total assets
- Be listed on a recognized stock exchange (like the LSE)

**How UK REITs work:**

1. The REIT raises capital by issuing shares
2. It acquires commercial or residential properties
3. Rental income is collected and managed professionally
4. At least 90% of rental profits are paid to shareholders as Property Income Distributions (PIDs)
5. Shareholders can trade shares on the stock market during trading hours

**Common UK REIT types:**

- **Retail REITs:** Shopping centres and high street properties (e.g., British Land)
- **Office REITs:** Commercial office buildings (e.g., Land Securities)
- **Residential REITs:** Build-to-rent apartments (e.g., Target Healthcare REIT)
- **Diversified REITs:** Mixed property portfolios (e.g., Segro)

**Example:** If you invest £10,000 in a UK REIT yielding 5%, you'd receive approximately £500 annually in dividends, paid quarterly. The share price itself can rise or fall based on property valuations and market sentiment.

## What Is Tokenized Real Estate?

Tokenized real estate converts property ownership into digital tokens on a blockchain. Each token represents fractional ownership of a specific property—not shares in a company that owns properties.

**How tokenization works:**

1. A property is purchased by a special purpose vehicle (SPV) or limited company
2. Ownership rights are divided into digital tokens (e.g., 10,000 tokens at £50 each for a £500,000 property)
3. Tokens are issued to investors on a blockchain platform
4. Rental income is distributed to token holders, typically in stablecoins or bank transfers
5. Investors can trade tokens on secondary markets (when available)

**Key differences from REITs:**

- **Direct ownership:** You own a fraction of a specific property, not shares in a diversified fund
- **Lower minimums:** Entry can start at £50-£500 versus £100+ for REITs (plus broker fees)
- **24/7 trading:** Blockchain-based secondary markets operate outside stock exchange hours
- **Transparent records:** Ownership and transactions are recorded on-chain
- **Automated distributions:** Smart contracts can handle rental payouts automatically

**Example:** A £300,000 Manchester apartment is tokenized into 6,000 tokens at £50 each. You buy 100 tokens (£5,000 investment). The property generates £1,500 monthly rent. After expenses, £1,000 is distributed to token holders. You receive your proportional share: (£5,000 / £300,000) × £1,000 = £16.67 monthly.

## Liquidity: Which Is More Liquid?

**REITs win on established liquidity.**

UK REITs trade on the London Stock Exchange during market hours (8am-4:30pm, Monday-Friday). You can sell shares instantly at market price through any broker. Settlement takes T+2 (two business days).

**Tokenized real estate liquidity is improving but limited.**

Secondary markets exist on some platforms, but:
- Trading volume is lower than REITs
- Buyers must be found on the same platform
- Some platforms impose lock-up periods (3-12 months)
- 24/7 trading is theoretical—actual liquidity depends on active buyers

**Reality check:** A £10,000 REIT position can be sold instantly during market hours. A £10,000 token position may take days or weeks to find a buyer, depending on platform activity.

## Minimum Investment: Which Is More Accessible?

**Tokenized real estate wins on accessibility.**

**UK REIT minimums:**
- Share prices range from £0.50 to £5+ per share
- Practical minimum: £100-£500 (to build meaningful position)
- Broker fees may apply (£5-£15 per trade)

**Tokenized property minimums:**
- Individual tokens: £10-£100 each
- Platform minimums: £50-£500 per property
- No broker fees (platform fees built into spreads)

**Example:** With £500, you could:
- Buy shares in 1-2 UK REITs (plus broker fees)
- Own tokens in 1-3 individual properties (diversified across locations)

## Returns: REITs vs Tokenized Property

**REIT returns are predictable but moderate.**

UK REITs historically deliver:
- **Dividend yield:** 3-6% annually
- **Capital appreciation:** 2-5% annually (long-term average)
- **Total return:** 5-11% annually (highly variable by year)

**Tokenized property targets higher yields but with less track record.**

Projected returns:
- **Rental yield:** 5-8% annually (property-specific)
- **Capital appreciation:** 0-5% annually (depends on local market)
- **Total return:** 5-13% annually (theoretical, limited historical data)

**Why tokenized yields can be higher:**
- No REIT-level corporate overhead
- Direct ownership eliminates fund management layers
- Residential properties often yield more than commercial REIT holdings
- Platforms compete on yield to attract investors

**Caveat:** Tokenized real estate has a shorter track record. REIT data spans decades; tokenization is still proving long-term performance.

## Tax Treatment: UK REITs vs Tokenized Real Estate

**REIT taxation is well-established.**

**UK REIT dividends (PIDs):**
- Treated as property income, not dividend income
- Basic rate taxpayers: 20% tax (often withheld at source)
- Higher rate taxpayers: 40% tax
- Additional rate taxpayers: 45% tax
- ISA/SIPP wrappers can shelter PIDs from tax

**Capital gains on REIT shares:**
- Subject to Capital Gains Tax (CGT)
- Annual CGT allowance: £3,000 (2025/26)
- Rate: 10% (basic) or 20% (higher/additional)

**Tokenized property taxation is less clear but follows similar principles.**

**Rental income from tokens:**
- Treated as property income
- Must be declared on Self Assessment tax return
- Taxed at marginal income tax rate (20%, 40%, or 45%)
- No tax withheld at source (your responsibility to declare)

**Capital gains on token sales:**
- Subject to Capital Gains Tax
- Same CGT allowance and rates as REITs
- Cryptoasset classification may apply (HMRC guidance evolving)

**Key difference:** REIT PIDs often have tax withheld automatically. Token rental income requires self-declaration via Self Assessment.

## Risk Comparison: What Can Go Wrong?

### REIT Risks

**Market risk:** REIT share prices fluctuate with stock market sentiment, not just property values.

**Interest rate risk:** Rising rates increase borrowing costs for REITs and make bonds more attractive relative to dividend yields.

**Property market risk:** Vacancies, rent reductions, or falling valuations directly impact dividends and share prices.

**Concentration risk:** Some REITs focus on specific sectors (e.g., retail, offices) that may underperform.

**Liquidity risk (non-traded REITs):** Non-listed REITs have limited redemption options and lock-up periods.

### Tokenized Real Estate Risks

**All REIT risks above, plus:**

**Technology risk:** Smart contract bugs, platform outages, or blockchain vulnerabilities could affect ownership records or distributions.

**Regulatory uncertainty:** FCA cryptoasset rules are evolving. Future regulation could impact token structure, taxation, or trading.

**Platform risk:** If the tokenization platform fails, secondary trading and distributions could be disrupted.

**Limited track record:** Tokenized real estate lacks decades of performance data. Long-term risks are not fully understood.

**Custody risk:** You're responsible for securing digital tokens (wallet management, private keys). Loss means loss of ownership.

**Secondary market risk:** Low trading volume means you may not find buyers when you want to sell.

## Which Should You Choose in 2026?

### Choose UK REITs If:

- ✅ You prioritize established liquidity and instant exits
- ✅ You want instant diversification across multiple properties
- ✅ You prefer professional management with decades of track record
- ✅ You want to hold investments in an ISA or SIPP
- ✅ You're comfortable with stock market trading hours
- ✅ You want clear, well-understood tax treatment

### Choose Tokenized Real Estate If:

- ✅ You want direct ownership in specific properties
- ✅ You're targeting higher rental yields (5-8%+)
- ✅ You prefer lower minimum investments (£50-£500)
- ✅ You want monthly rental distributions instead of quarterly
- ✅ You're comfortable with evolving technology and regulation
- ✅ You believe in blockchain's long-term role in real estate

### Consider Both (Diversified Approach):

Many investors use both:
- **Core holding (60-70%):** UK REITs for stability and liquidity
- **Satellite positions (30-40%):** Tokenized properties for yield and diversification

**Example portfolio with £10,000:**
- £6,000 in 2-3 diversified UK REITs (instant diversification, easy to manage)
- £4,000 across 4-8 tokenized properties (higher yield, property-specific exposure)

## The Bottom Line

REITs and tokenized real estate aren't mutually exclusive. Both offer property exposure without landlord responsibilities. The right choice depends on your priorities:

- **Liquidity and track record:** REITs win
- **Yield and accessibility:** Tokenization wins
- **Diversification:** REITs win (instant), tokenization requires more capital
- **Control and transparency:** Tokenization wins (property-specific, on-chain records)

For most UK investors starting in 2026, a blended approach makes sense: use REITs as your foundation, then allocate a portion to tokenized properties for yield enhancement and portfolio diversification.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I hold tokenized real estate in an ISA?

No. Currently, tokenized properties cannot be held in a Stocks & Shares ISA or Innovative Finance ISA. UK REITs can be held in ISAs, sheltering dividends and capital gains from tax. This is a significant advantage for REITs among UK investors.

### Are tokenized real estate platforms FCA-regulated?

It depends. Some platforms operate under FCA cryptoasset registration, which covers anti-money laundering compliance but does not equate to full FCA authorization for investment services. Always check a platform's FCA registration status and understand what protections apply. REITs, by contrast, are fully regulated investment products.

### What happens if a tokenization platform shuts down?

This is a key risk. If the platform fails, you still legally own the underlying property shares (held in an SPV), but:
- Secondary trading may stop
- Rental distribution systems could be disrupted
- You may need to work with administrators to reclaim ownership or sell your position

Always research platform backing, custody arrangements, and contingency plans before investing.

### Do REITs pay more tax than tokenized properties?

UK REITs don't pay corporation tax on rental income (if they distribute 90%+ as PIDs). Investors pay income tax on PIDs. Tokenized properties held in SPVs may pay corporation tax at the company level, then investors pay income tax on distributions. The total tax burden varies by structure—consult a tax advisor for your situation.

### Can I lose all my money in either option?

Yes. Both carry risk of capital loss:
- **REITs:** Share prices can fall significantly during property downturns (e.g., UK REITs fell 40-60% in 2008 and 2020)
- **Tokenized properties:** Property values can fall, platforms can fail, or technology issues could affect ownership records

Never invest money you cannot afford to lose in either option.
