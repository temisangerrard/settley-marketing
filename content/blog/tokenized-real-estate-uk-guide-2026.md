## What Is Tokenized Real Estate?

**Tokenized real estate** converts property ownership into digital tokens on a blockchain. Each token represents a fraction of the property's value and entitles holders to proportional rental income and capital appreciation.

### Traditional Property Investment

```
£50,000 deposit → Mortgage approval → Buy entire property → Manage tenants → Collect rent → Pay maintenance → Sell in 3-6 months (5-8% fees)
```

### Tokenized Property Investment

```
£100 → Buy tokens → Receive monthly distributions → Sell tokens anytime (2-3% fees)
```

### Key Differences

| Factor | Traditional | Tokenized |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| Minimum | £50,000+ | £100 |
| Mortgage | Required | Not needed |
| Management | Your responsibility | Platform-managed |
| Liquidity | Low (3-6 months to sell) | Medium (secondary market) |
| Diversification | Low (1-2 properties) | High (10+ properties) |
| Transaction costs | 5-8% | 2-3% |

## How Tokenization Works

### Step 1: Property Acquisition

Settley acquires a UK rental property (e.g., £200,000 terraced house in Manchester).

**Due diligence:**
- Property valuation (RICS-certified)
- Rental yield analysis (target: 6%+ gross yield)
- Tenant demand assessment (local rental market)
- Legal checks (title, planning, compliance)

### Step 2: SPV Structure

The property is placed in a Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV) — typically a UK Limited Company.

**Why an SPV?**
- Legal separation from platform (investor protection)
- Clear ownership structure (shares = tokens)
- Tax efficiency (corporation tax vs. income tax)
- FCA compliance (restricted mass market investments)

### Step 3: Tokenization

The SPV's shares are tokenized into ERC-20 tokens on the Base blockchain.

**Token economics:**
- Total supply: 200,000 tokens (for £200k property)
- Token price: £1 per token
- Minimum purchase: 100 tokens (£100)
- Distribution: Monthly rental income (proportional to holdings)

### Step 4: Investor Purchase

Investors buy tokens through Settley's platform.

**Investor rights:**
- Proportional ownership of SPV (and underlying property)
- Monthly rental distributions (via smart contract)
- Voting rights on major decisions (sale, refinance)
- Secondary market trading (sell tokens to other investors)

### Step 5: Rental Income Distribution

Tenants pay rent to the SPV. After expenses (management, maintenance, insurance), net income is distributed to token holders.

**Distribution flow:**
```
Tenant rent → SPV bank account → Expenses deducted → Smart contract distributes → Investor wallets
```

**Frequency:** Monthly  
**Method:** Stablecoin (USDC) or GBP bank transfer  
**Minimum:** £10 (threshold for distribution)

### Step 6: Exit (Secondary Market)

Token holders can sell their tokens on Settley's secondary market.

**Process:**
1. List tokens for sale (set price or market order)
2. Buyer purchases tokens
3. Smart contract transfers ownership
4. Seller receives proceeds (minus 2-3% fee)

**Liquidity note:** Early stages may have limited buyers. As the marketplace grows, liquidity improves.

## FCA Regulation (UK-Specific)

The FCA regulates tokenized real estate under cryptoasset rules (CP25/28, CP25/40, CP25/41).

### Key Regulations

**1. Financial Promotion Rules**
- All marketing must be clear, fair, and not misleading
- Risk warnings required ("capital at risk", "not FSCS protected")
- Appropriateness assessment for investors

**2. Cryptoasset Registration**
- Platforms must register with FCA
- AML/KYC compliance required
- Ongoing reporting obligations

**3. Restricted Mass Market Investments (RMMI)**
- Tokenized real estate classified as RMMI
- Only certified high-net-worth or sophisticated investors can invest
- OR: Platform provides appropriateness assessment for retail investors

### What This Means for Investors

**Protections:**
- Clear risk disclosures
- Platform oversight (FCA registration)
- AML/KYC checks (prevents fraud)
- Appropriateness testing (ensures you understand risks)

**Limitations:**
- Not FSCS protected (no government guarantee)
- High-risk classification (may lose capital)
- Retail investor restrictions (appropriateness test required)

- FCA cryptoasset registration (pending)
- Appropriateness assessments for all investors
- Clear risk warnings (every page, every email)
- AML/KYC verification (identity, source of funds)

## Risks to Understand

### 1. Capital Risk

**Property values can fall.** You may lose some or all of your invested capital.

**Mitigation:** Diversification (10+ properties), long-term hold (5+ years), conservative valuations.

### 2. Liquidity Risk

**Secondary market may be thin.** Selling tokens may take days/weeks, especially in early stages.

**Mitigation:** Hold for income (not quick flips), platform buyback program (under consideration), gradual marketplace growth.

### 3. Platform Risk

**Settley could fail operationally.** Poor management, fraud, or insolvency could impact returns.

**Mitigation:** FCA oversight, SPV structure (assets separate from platform), transparent reporting, third-party audits.

### 4. Tenant Risk

**Tenants can default.** Void periods reduce rental income.

**Mitigation:** Professional property management, tenant screening, rental guarantee insurance, diversification (multiple properties/tenants).

### 5. Regulatory Risk

**FCA rules could change.** New regulations could impact platform operations or investor eligibility.

**Mitigation:** Proactive compliance, legal counsel, industry advocacy (UK PropTech Association), flexible platform architecture.

### 6. Technology Risk

**Smart contracts could have bugs.** Blockchain technology is evolving.

**Mitigation:** Third-party audits (CertiK, OpenZeppelin), bug bounty program, gradual rollout, insurance coverage (under consideration).

## Tax Considerations

### Income Tax

**Rental distributions** are taxed as property income.

**Tax bands (2026/27):**
- Basic rate (20%): £12,571 - £50,270
- Higher rate (40%): £50,271 - £125,140
- Additional rate (45%): £125,140+

**Personal Savings Allowance:**
- Basic rate: £1,000 tax-free
- Higher rate: £500 tax-free
- Additional rate: £0 tax-free

### Capital Gains Tax

**Token appreciation** is subject to CGT on disposal.

**Annual exempt amount (2026/27):** £3,000  
**CGT rates:**
- Basic rate: 18% (property)
- Higher/additional rate: 24% (property)

### ISA/SIPP Eligibility

**Current status:** Tokenized real estate is NOT currently ISA or SIPP eligible.

**Future possibility:** FCA may approve ISA wrapper for tokenized real estate (industry lobbying ongoing).

### Stamp Duty Land Tax (SDLT)

**Token purchases:** No SDLT on token purchases (you're buying shares in SPV, not direct property).

**SPV level:** Settley pays SDLT on property acquisitions (3% additional dwellings supplement).

**Impact:** Already factored into token pricing (no additional cost to investors).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is tokenized real estate legal in the UK?

**Yes.** Tokenized real estate is legal under current UK law. Properties are held in SPVs (Limited Companies), and tokens represent shares in the SPV. The FCA regulates platforms under cryptoasset rules (CP25/28, CP25/40, CP25/41).

### Is my investment protected?

**No government guarantee.** Tokenized real estate is NOT protected by FSCS (Financial Services Compensation Scheme). You may lose some or all of your invested capital.

**Protections in place:**
- SPV structure (assets separate from platform)
- FCA oversight (platform registration, reporting)
- Third-party audits (financials, smart contracts)
- Transparent reporting (monthly property performance)

### Can I lose more than I invest?

**No.** Your liability is limited to your investment. You cannot lose more than you put in (unlike leveraged buy-to-let with a mortgage).

### How do I sell my tokens?

**Secondary market.** List tokens for sale on Settley's marketplace. Set your price or accept market orders. Buyers purchase tokens, smart contract transfers ownership, you receive proceeds (minus 2-3% fee).

**Liquidity note:** Early stages may have limited buyers. Plan to hold for income (5+ years), not quick flips.

### What happens if Settley goes bust?

**SPV protection.** Properties are held in separate SPVs (Limited Companies). If Settley fails, SPVs continue operating. Token holders still own shares in SPVs and receive rental income.

**Platform failure scenario:**
1. Settley enters administration
2. SPVs continue operating (separate legal entities)
3. Token holders vote on new platform/manager
4. Rental distributions continue (via smart contract)

### Can I visit my property?

**No.** You own tokens (shares in SPV), not direct property. Tenants have legal tenancy rights. Property visits would violate tenant privacy.

**Alternative:** Virtual tours, photos, monthly updates, annual reports.

### Do I get voting rights?

**Yes.** Token holders can vote on major decisions:
- Property sale (majority vote)
- Refinancing (majority vote)
- Platform change (majority vote)
- Fee changes (majority vote)

**Voting mechanism:** On-chain governance (snapshot voting, token-weighted)

### What's the fee structure?

**Tokenization fee:** 2-3% (one-time, on purchase)  
**Management fee:** 1% annual (on AUM)  
**Secondary market fee:** 2-3% (on sale)  
**Withdrawal fee:** Free (bank transfer), 1% (crypto)

**Example:**
```
Invest: £1,000
Tokenization fee (3%): £30
Net investment: £970

Annual management fee (1%): £9.70/year

Sell after 1 year (£1,050 value)
Secondary market fee (3%): £31.50
Net proceeds: £1,018.50
```
