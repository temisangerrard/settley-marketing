Tokenized Real Estate vs REITs: An Honest Comparison (2026)

Both give you property exposure without buying whole buildings. But they're fundamentally different. Here's how to choose.

Key Takeaways
  • REITs are publicly traded companies (stock market); tokenized property is direct ownership via blockchain tokens.
  • REITs offer daily liquidity; tokenized property has limited or no secondary market (3-7 year hold typical).
  • REITs are diversified (50+ properties); tokenized lets you pick specific properties.
  • REITs are FCA-regulated (full); tokenized platforms face new FCA regime (October 2027).
  • REIT returns: 5-10% p.a. (dividends + appreciation); tokenized: 6-12% p.a. (rent + appreciation).
  • Choose REITs for liquidity and diversification; choose tokenized for control and higher potential returns.

The Quick Answer

Choose REITs if you want: Daily liquidity, instant diversification, FCA protection, and don't mind lower returns (5-10% p.a.).

Choose tokenized property if you want: Direct property ownership, choice of specific assets, higher potential returns (6-12% p.a.), and can lock up capital for 3-7 years.

They're both valid. They're both property. But they solve different problems for different investors.

What Are REITs?

The Basics

REITs (Real Estate Investment Trusts) are companies that own and operate income-generating property. You buy shares on the stock market — just like buying Tesla or Barclays stock.

Key features:

  • Publicly traded: Listed on London Stock Exchange (LSE)
  • Tax-efficient: REITs don't pay corporation tax on rental profits (must distribute 90% to shareholders)
  • Professional management: Team of property experts makes all decisions
  • Diversified portfolios: Single REIT may own 50-100+ properties

How You Make Money

  • Dividends: 90% of rental income distributed to shareholders (typically 3-6% yield)
  • Capital appreciation: Share price increases as property portfolio grows in value (2-5% p.a. historical)
  • Total return: 5-10% p.a. (combined dividends + appreciation)

Popular UK REITs

  • British Land (BLND): Retail and office (£5.7B portfolio)
  • Land Securities (LAND): Commercial and residential (£6.8B portfolio)
  • Segro (SGRO): Warehouses and logistics (£16.5B portfolio)
  • Tritax Big Box (BBOX): Large-format retail warehouses (£2.4B portfolio)

What Is Tokenized Real Estate?

The Basics

Tokenized real estate uses blockchain technology to represent ownership of individual properties. A property is purchased by an SPV (Special Purpose Vehicle — a UK Limited Company), which issues digital tokens representing shares.

Key features:

  • Direct ownership: You own tokens representing a specific property, not a diversified portfolio
  • Blockchain recorded: Ownership is recorded on a blockchain (Ethereum, Polygon, etc.)
  • Lower minimums: Typically £500-£1,000 vs. £50k+ for traditional buy-to-let
  • Professional management: Platform handles tenants, maintenance, compliance

How You Make Money

  • Rental distributions: Monthly or quarterly rent payments (4-7% yield)
  • Capital appreciation: Property value increases over time (2-5% p.a.)
  • Total return: 6-12% p.a. (combined rent + appreciation)

UK Platforms (Emerging)

  • Settley: Tokenized residential properties (waitlist phase)
  • Bricksave: International properties available to UK investors
  • Uprise: Commercial property tokens
Regulatory Note
The FCA's new cryptoasset regime (October 2027) will require tokenized property platforms to be FCA-authorised. Check platform compliance status before investing.

The Trade-Offs: What You Gain and Lose

REITs: What You Gain

  • ✅ Liquidity: Sell anytime during market hours (90%+ of capital accessible within days)
  • ✅ Diversification: Single REIT holds 50-100+ properties across sectors and regions
  • ✅ Regulation: Full FCA oversight, listed company disclosure requirements
  • ✅ Track record: 30+ years of UK REIT performance data
  • ✅ Tax efficiency: ISA-eligible, dividend tax allowances apply

REITs: What You Lose

  • ❌ Control: You can't choose which properties the REIT buys or sells
  • ❌ Direct ownership: You own shares in a company, not bricks and mortar
  • ❌ Lower returns: Diversification reduces both risk and upside potential
  • ❌ Market volatility: Share price fluctuates daily with stock market sentiment

Tokenized Property: What You Gain

  • ✅ Direct ownership: You own a specific property (or share of one)
  • ✅ Choice: Pick properties by location, type, yield, and strategy
  • ✅ Higher returns: Concentrated risk = concentrated reward potential
  • ✅ Transparency: Blockchain records show exact ownership and distributions
  • ✅ Lower minimums: £500 gets you in vs. £50k+ for traditional buy-to-let

Tokenized Property: What You Lose

  • ❌ Liquidity: Most platforms have no secondary market (capital locked for 3-7 years)
  • ❌ Diversification: Must manually spread capital across multiple properties
  • ❌ Track record: Emerging technology (most platforms launched 2020-2025)
  • ❌ Regulatory certainty: FCA regime not fully implemented until October 2027
  • ❌ Platform risk: If the platform fails, exits become complicated

When to Choose REITs

REITs are better if you:

  • Want to be able to sell within days (not years)
  • Prefer "set it and forget it" investing (no property selection needed)
  • Want instant diversification with a single purchase
  • Value regulatory protection and long track records
  • Want to use your ISA allowance (tax-free growth)
  • Are comfortable with stock market volatility

Ideal REIT Investor Profile

  • Investment horizon: 5+ years (but wants option to sell sooner)
  • Risk tolerance: Medium (comfortable with 20-30% drawdowns in crises)
  • Capital: £1,000-£100,000+
  • Involvement: Passive (no interest in property selection)

When to Choose Tokenized Property

Tokenized property is better if you:

  • Can lock up capital for 3-7 years without needing access
  • Want to choose specific properties (location, type, strategy)
  • Are comfortable with emerging technology and regulatory evolution
  • Want higher potential returns (and accept higher risk)
  • Prefer direct property ownership over company shares
  • Want to start with £500-£5,000 (not £50k+)

Ideal Tokenized Property Investor Profile

  • Investment horizon: 5-10 years (capital locked up)
  • Risk tolerance: Medium-High (comfortable with platform and technology risk)
  • Capital: £500-£50,000
  • Involvement: Active (wants to choose properties)

A Third Option: Do Both

You don't have to choose one or the other. Many investors use a core-satellite approach:

  • Core (60-70%): REITs for liquidity, diversification, and stability
  • Satellite (30-40%): Tokenized property for higher returns and direct ownership

  • ✅ 60% liquid (can sell REITs anytime)
  • ✅ 40% direct property ownership
  • ✅ Diversification across 4+ properties
  • ✅ Blend of stability (REITs) and upside (tokenized)

Risks to Understand (Both)

Capital at Risk
All property investment carries risk. You could lose some or all of your capital. Past performance does not indicate future results. Never invest money you cannot afford to lose.

REIT-Specific Risks

  • Market risk: Share prices can fall 30-50% in crises (2008: UK REITs fell 60%)
  • Interest rate risk: Higher rates make REIT dividends less attractive vs. bonds
  • Property risk: REIT portfolios can lose value if property markets decline
  • Management risk: Poor acquisition or disposal decisions affect returns

Tokenized Property Risks

  • Liquidity risk: May not be able to sell when you want (or at all)
  • Platform risk: If platform fails, your investment may be tied up in administration
  • Technology risk: Smart contract bugs, blockchain issues, or wallet security
  • Regulatory risk: Future rules may affect platform operations or your rights
  • Property risk: Same as REITs (void periods, value declines, maintenance costs)

Bottom Line

REITs and tokenized real estate are both valid ways to invest in property without buying whole buildings.

REITs are the mature, liquid, diversified option — ideal for passive investors who want stock market accessibility with property exposure.

Tokenized property is the emerging, higher-return, direct-ownership option — ideal for investors who want to choose specific properties and can lock up capital for years.

Neither is "better." They solve different problems for different investors at different stages of their journey.

If you value liquidity and diversification above all: choose REITs.

If you value control and higher returns (and can accept illiquidity): choose tokenized.

If you want both: do a core-satellite split (60% REITs, 40% tokenized).
