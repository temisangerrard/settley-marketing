# Investor Sourcing Rubric

Purpose: help Mira find investors who are actually plausible fits for Settley, Arena Play, and other active ventures before spending time on contact discovery or outreach.

## Core rule

This is a portfolio-level sourcing system.

- Apply the same workflow across active ventures.
- Change the thesis filter per venture.
- Do not mix multiple venture narratives in the same first-touch outreach unless there is a very specific reason.
- Build or maintain separate target lists per venture when the investor universe meaningfully changes.

## Venture thesis filters

### Settley

Settley is easiest to underwrite for investors who can understand at least two of these frames:

1. Fintech infrastructure for investing access
2. Proptech / real estate workflow innovation
3. Real-world assets / tokenization / digital asset rails
4. Marketplace or access-layer business model
5. Regulated category with strong trust and distribution requirements

### Arena Play

Arena Play is easiest to underwrite for investors who can understand at least two of these frames:

1. Consumer crypto or onchain gaming
2. Gaming infrastructure or game platforms
3. Appchains, protocol ecosystems, or developer platforms
4. AI agents, autonomous systems, or programmable gameplay loops
5. High-engagement consumer products with embedded financial or token mechanics

## Ideal target profile

Prioritize investors who match most of the following:

- Pre-seed or seed behavior is explicit
- UK and/or US market understanding
- History in one or more relevant venture buckets: fintech, proptech, gaming, appchains, marketplaces, infrastructure, AI agents, or digital assets
- Comfortable with regulated or operationally heavy businesses
- Known to back category-creating or “non-obvious software” companies
- Has a named partner, principal, or GP who can plausibly own the conversation

## Anti-targets

Do not prioritize these by default:

- Generic `info@`, `hello@`, or contact-form-only outreach
- Late-stage funds with no clear pre-seed behavior
- Funds with only generic B2B SaaS interest and no meaningful overlap with the active venture thesis
- Corporate “innovation” teams without a clear investor or partner contact
- Investors whose thesis is clearly outside the current venture's category logic

## Scorecard

Score each prospect out of 25.

### 1. Thesis fit (0-5)
- `5`: explicit overlap with the current venture's thesis buckets
- `3`: adjacent and plausible, but not explicit
- `1`: weak and mostly aspirational fit
- `0`: no meaningful overlap

### 2. Stage fit (0-5)
- `5`: clearly pre-seed / seed
- `3`: seed / Series A crossover
- `1`: mostly later-stage
- `0`: wrong stage

### 3. Geography fit (0-5)
- `5`: strong UK/US relevance
- `3`: Europe or global with likely UK/US openness
- `1`: geography mismatch
- `0`: no useful market overlap

### 4. Contact quality (0-5)
- `5`: named partner / GP / principal on official site
- `3`: named investor exists but contact path is weak
- `1`: only generic channel exists
- `0`: no accountable person identified

### 5. Angle quality (0-5)
- `5`: one clean sentence explains why the current venture belongs in their thesis
- `3`: fit exists but story needs stretching
- `1`: weak angle
- `0`: no real angle

## Decision thresholds

- `21-25`: Tier A, source immediately
- `16-20`: Tier B, keep active
- `11-15`: Tier C, only if the top list is exhausted
- `0-10`: reject

## Outreach order

1. Find a fund with explicit thesis overlap
2. Find a named investor on the official site
3. Write the one-line venture-specific angle for that person
4. Score the prospect
5. Only after score `>= 16`, look for contact path

## Named-contact rules

- Prefer GP, managing partner, partner, principal, or clearly investing associate
- Avoid generic team addresses unless the fund explicitly asks for founder applications there
- If a fund has a formal application path and a named investor, use both only when the target scores high enough

## Angle templates

Use one primary angle per investor:

### Settley

- `Fintech access`: “Settley turns property investing into a software-native financial product.”
- `Proptech infrastructure`: “Settley is building the access and distribution layer for fractional property ownership.”
- `RWA / digital assets`: “Settley applies tokenization rails to a real asset category with large retail demand.”
- `Marketplace`: “Settley lowers the minimum and complexity barrier for a historically inaccessible asset.”
- `Regulated software`: “Settley sits at the intersection of trust, compliance, asset access, and distribution.”

### Arena Play

- `Onchain gaming`: “Arena Play is building a crypto-native gaming loop where agents and players compete in live onchain formats.”
- `Appchain / protocol ecosystem`: “Arena Play is a high-frequency consumer use case for appchain infrastructure and programmable game economies.”
- `AI agents`: “Arena Play turns autonomous agents into an interactive product layer instead of a backend abstraction.”
- `Developer platform`: “Arena Play can evolve into both a game and a testbed for agent behavior, competitions, and tooling.”
- `Consumer crypto`: “Arena Play packages crypto rails into a replayable, spectator-friendly consumer experience.”

## Fast rejection heuristics

Reject quickly if:

- the case depends only on “they invest in startups”
- there is no named investor to anchor the approach
- the fund looks too broad and too busy to care about a $200K pre-seed
- the thesis fit depends on three layers of interpretation
- the target only fits a different venture and Mira is forcing crossover without a real bridge

## Multi-venture workflow

When Temisan has multiple active ventures:

1. Pick the venture first
2. Apply the thesis filter for that venture
3. Build a venture-specific angle
4. Keep the target list separate unless the investor truly spans both worlds

Only use a combined narrative when the overlap is genuine, for example:
- software infrastructure that spans both consumer crypto and real-world assets
- an investor already known to back multi-vertical platform companies
- a relationship that explicitly asked about the broader portfolio story

## Working rule for Mira

Strong investor sourcing is not “find emails.” It is:

`fit first -> named owner second -> angle third -> contact path last`
